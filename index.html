Taylor Kutra

About

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at the University of Rochester, working with Dr. Dominique Segura-Cox on the PRODIGE Large Program using NOEMA. Broadly, my research aims to understand the conditions necessary for planet formation by characterizing planet-forming circumstellar disks in the tumultuous stellar environments of multiple star systems and around white dwarfs, the remnants of Sun-like stars. I investigate how much material remains in these disks, where it is located, and how long it persists, to explore the resilience of planet-forming disks and their broader implications for star formation and the fates of planetary systems. My work makes use of high- and low-resolution radio surveys of young stellar systems and their environment, as well as adaptive optics imaging and high resolution spectroscopy and is supported by top-ranked, observing programs at Gemini South and Magellan for which I am the PI. Central to my program is student participation: by spanning multiple wavelengths, techniques, and systems, my research offers a wide range of opportunities for students to meaningfully engage at any stage of the research process.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at Lowell Observatory and I completed my doctorate in the Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics at the University of Toronto under the supervision of Dr. Yanqin Wu. I attended Canada's only not-for-profit, secular, liberal arts and sciences university, Quest University Canada, for my undergraduate degree. When I am not doing astronomy, I enjoy being in the outdoors, and taking care of my dog, Shanty. I am committed to making astronomy accessible and inclusive for diverse audiences.
