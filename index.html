About

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at the University of Rochester, working with Dr. Dominique Segura-Cox on the PRODIGE Large Program using NOEMA. I study a variety of problems in planet formation from both a theoretical and observational perspective. My research focuses on how the star forming environment shapes the protoplanetary disk and how that, in turn, may affect forming planets.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow at Lowell Observatory and I completed my doctorate in the Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics at the University of Toronto under the supervision of Dr. Yanqin Wu. My thesis research focuses on modeling the irradiation instability using Athena++ to form gaps and rings that are observed in ALMA discs. I attended Canada's only not-for-profit, secular, liberal arts and sciences university, Quest University Canada, for my undergraduate degree.

When I am not doing astronomy, I enjoy being in the outdoors, and taking care of my dog, Shanty. I am committed to making astronomy accessible and inclusive for diverse audiences.
