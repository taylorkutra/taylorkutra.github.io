About

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at Lowell Observatory, working under Dr. Lisa Prato and the Disks and Exoplanets in Flagstaff Team (DEFT). I study a variety of problems in planet formation from both a theoretical and observational perspective. My research focuses on the protoplanetary disk and I am generally interested in how the protoplanetary disk can affect the planets that eventually form from it.

After studying at Quest University Canada, I completed my doctorate in the Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics at the University of Toronto under the supervision of Dr. Yanqin Wu. My thesis research focuses on modeling the irradiation instability using Athena++ to form gaps and rings that are observed in ALMA discs.

When I am not doing astronomy, I enjoy being in the outdoors, and taking care of my dog, Shanty. I am committed to making astronomy accessible and inclusive for diverse audiences.
